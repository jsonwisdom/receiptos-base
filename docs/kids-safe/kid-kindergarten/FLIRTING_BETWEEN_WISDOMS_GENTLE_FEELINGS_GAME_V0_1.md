## Purpose

Flirting Between Wisdoms is a gentle wisdom game about sharing, feelings, kindness, consent, noticing, and repair.

In this child-safe lane, “flirting” does not mean romance, sexuality, adult behavior, pressure, or secrets.

It means playful kindness between learning characters called Wisdoms.

## Child-Safe Definition

A Wisdom can show friendly care by:

- smiling kindly
- sharing a turn
- asking before joining
- noticing feelings
- saying thank you
- saying sorry
- giving space
- inviting repair

## Core Rule

Kindness is only kind when the other person is safe to say yes, no, or not now.

## Game Loop

```text
NOTICE → ASK → SHARE → LISTEN → RESPECT → REPAIR → RECEIPT
```

### Notice

What feeling do you see?

### Ask

Can I join? Can I share? Do you want help?

### Share

Offer a toy, word, turn, song, drawing, or idea.

### Listen

Hear yes, no, maybe, or not now.

### Respect

Stop, wait, or change when someone says no.

### Repair

If someone feels bad, fix one thing kindly.

### Receipt

Record what changed with a tiny receipt.

## Safe Phrases

- “Can I play too?”
- “Do you want a turn?”
- “It is okay to say no.”
- “I can wait.”
- “I am sorry. I can fix one thing.”
- “Thank you for telling me.”
- “Your feeling matters.”

## Unsafe Patterns

- pressuring someone after no
- teasing someone for feelings
- keeping a secret that hides harm
- touching without permission
- saying a child owes affection
- using romance or adult behavior
- using shame, dares, or exclusion
