## Protocol Invariant

A child has not truly learned until they can explain it safely in their own words, repair one mistake, and produce a small receipt of what changed.

## 1. Study — Meet the Idea

The child encounters the idea in its simplest, observable form.

- No authority.
- No memorization demand.
- Just meeting the idea.

Child prompt: “Can you show me?”

Invariant: First contact must be gentle, visible, replayable.

## 2. Learn — Try It With Help

The child experiments with the idea while supported.

- Mistakes expected.
- Guidance is optional, not hierarchical.

Child prompt: “Can I try?”

Invariant: Learning is participation, not obedience.

## 3. Practice — Make a Small Attempt

The child performs a tiny, safe action.

- One step.
- One attempt.
- No performance pressure.

Child prompt: “Let me try a small piece.”

Invariant: Practice is micro-scale, not evaluation.

## 4. Explain — Say It in Your Own Words

The child translates the idea into their own language.

- No jargon required.
- No adult phrasing required.
- No shame for imperfect wording.

Child prompt: “Can you say it your way?”

Invariant: Understanding is self-expression, not recitation.

## 5. Repair — Fix One Mistake Kindly

The child identifies one thing to improve.

- No shame.
- No harsh grading.
- Just gentle correction.

Child prompt: “Can you fix one thing?”

Invariant: Repair is kindness, not critique.

## 6. Receipt — Record What Changed

The child produces a tiny artifact showing the change.

Examples:

- a drawing
- a sentence
- a before/after
- a small diff
- a spoken explanation transcribed by an adult safely

Child prompt: “Can we make a receipt of what changed?”

Invariant: Receipts anchor continuity and prevent fake-green.

## 7. Teach — Help Someone Else

The child helps another child understand the idea.

- Peer-safe.
- No authority.
- No hierarchy.

Child prompt: “Can you help another kid understand?”

Invariant: Teaching is participation, not power.

## Replay-Safe Loop

```text
STUDY → LEARN → PRACTICE → EXPLAIN → REPAIR → RECEIPT → TEACH
```

## Kid Kuorum Requirement

Every teach-back event must include:

1. Child question or activity prompt
2. Evidence or observation note
3. Safety boundary check
4. Repair or learning path

No Kuorum means do not rule.

## Hard Boundaries

- No child wallets.
- No real money for kids.
- No financial PEPE.
- No private data.
- No birthdates.
- No phone numbers.
- No addresses.
- No school locations.
- No bullying.
- No humiliation games.
- No adult-centric correctness.
- No canonical authority claim.

## Ruling

Kid Kindergarten Teach-Back Protocols are bound under the Receipt-First Learning Charter.

Learning is legitimate only when a child can try, explain, repair, receipt, and teach safely.

Authority remains false.
