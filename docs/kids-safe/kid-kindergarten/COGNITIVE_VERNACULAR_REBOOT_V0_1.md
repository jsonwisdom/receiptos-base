## Purpose

Cognitive Vernacular is the child-safe language layer for naming thoughts, feelings, attempts, repairs, receipts, and teach-back moments.

This reboot makes the language gentle, simple, replayable, and safe for children.

It is not diagnosis, therapy, surveillance, grading, or authority.

## Core Rule

A child can name what happened without being shamed for what happened.

## Rebooted Vocabulary

### Thought Words

- “I noticed.”
- “I wondered.”
- “I guessed.”
- “I changed my mind.”
- “I need help.”

### Feeling Words

- “I feel happy.”
- “I feel unsure.”
- “I feel frustrated.”
- “I feel proud.”
- “I need a quiet beat.”

### Learning Words

- “I tried.”
- “I practiced.”
- “I made one mistake.”
- “I fixed one thing.”
- “I can say it my way.”

### Repair Words

- “I can try again.”
- “I can wait.”
- “I can say sorry.”
- “I can ask first.”
- “I can make it kinder.”

### Receipt Words

- “Here is what changed.”
- “Before, I thought this.”
- “After, I learned this.”
- “My receipt is a drawing.”
- “My receipt is one sentence.”

## Child-Safe Learning Loop

```text
NOTICE → NAME → TRY → REPAIR → RECEIPT → TEACH
```

## AI-Enabled Rules

AI may:

- suggest gentle words
- explain feelings at kindergarten level
- help turn a mistake into a repair sentence
- help create a public-safe learning receipt
- help a child say the idea in their own words

AI may not:

- diagnose children
- label a child as broken, bad, lazy, or wrong
- collect private mental-health data
- pressure a child to reveal secrets
- replace adult care or guardian judgment
- become authority

## Kid Kuorum Requirement

Every Cognitive Vernacular lesson must include:

1. Child question or activity prompt
2. Evidence or observation note
3. Safety boundary check
4. Repair or learning path

No Kuorum means do not rule.
