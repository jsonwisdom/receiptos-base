## Purpose

Super Secret Sister Syntax is a playful pretend-language protocol for Grade 1 learners.

It teaches children how to use kindness, privacy, receipts, and repair when they make secret codes, sister signs, classroom symbols, or imagination-language.

“Secret” means playful and safe.

It does not mean hiding harm, private data, adult secrets, unsafe behavior, or authority.

## Core Rule

A secret code is safe only when it protects kindness and privacy without hiding danger.

## Wisdom Grade 1 Syntax Loop

```text
SEE → SAY → SHARE SAFELY → REPAIR → RECEIPT
```

### See

Notice the symbol, sign, rhythm, word, or pattern.

### Say

Explain what it means in simple words.

### Share Safely

Share only safe, public, non-private meaning.

### Repair

Fix one confusing or unkind part.

### Receipt

Record what changed with a tiny receipt.

## Child-Safe Examples

Safe syntax examples:

- “Sun means kind start.”
- “Star means try again.”
- “Purple bench means wait and breathe.”
- “Goblin grin means mistake became learning.”
- “Sister signal means help kindly.”

Unsafe syntax examples:

- real passwords
- addresses
- phone numbers
- school locations
- birthdates
- private family information
- hidden bullying
- secret instructions to exclude, shame, or trick another child

## Sister Safety Rule

Sister syntax must never expose, pressure, identify, or control a real sibling or child.

“Sister” is a role of care, not ownership or authority.
