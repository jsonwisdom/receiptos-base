## Constitutional Purpose

Anchor all child-safe learning systems to a single invariant:

> A child does not need to obey the answer.  
> A child learns by trying, explaining, repairing, and making a receipt.

This is the governing definition of learning.

Everything downstream must comply.

## Charter Invariants

### Study

A child must be able to meet the idea safely.

### Learn

A child must be able to try the idea with help.

### Practice

A child must be able to make a small attempt without fear.

### Explain

A child must be able to say the idea in their own words.

### Repair

A child must be able to fix one mistake kindly.

### Receipt

A child must be able to make a record of what changed.

### Teach

A child must be able to help another child understand.

## Core Ruling

Learning is not obedience.

Learning is participation, expression, repair, and receipt.

A child is considered to have learned only when:

1. They can attempt the idea.
2. They can explain it in their own words.
3. They can repair one mistake.
4. They can produce a receipt of what changed.

## Governance Order

1. Receipt-First Learning Charter
2. Kid Kindergarten Teach-Back Protocols
3. Teach-Back Cards
4. Kid Kourt Replay Rules
5. Explain-Repair Curriculum

No layer may violate the charter.

## Constitutional Effects

- No authority-based correctness.
- No fake-green “you got it” without a receipt.
- No memorization-only learning.
- No opaque evaluation.
- No adult-centric phrasing.
- No hierarchy in teaching.

Authority remains false.
